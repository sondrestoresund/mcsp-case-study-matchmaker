# Case Study Label Audit v2

Method used:
- Reviewed exact case study copy only: title, market, result, summary, story.
- Applied precision-over-recall rules.
- Removed labels that depended on inference rather than explicit support.
- Performed a self-audit after each entry: would this label improve or hurt precision in a strict filter UI?

Fields:
- Industry
- Challenge
- Channel
- Region
- Outcome
- Self-audit note

## 1. Rakuten
**Title:** TikTok Creative Optimization  
**Market:** US  
**Result:** -35% CPI, -31% CPA, +77% SKAN click conversion rate  
**Summary:** Used TikTok Creative Exchange and Automatic Creative Optimization to test 40+ permutations and find the best-performing creative mix.  
**Story:** Rakuten wanted TikTok to work like a serious acquisition channel, not just a place to post brand creative. We brought in TikTok Creative Exchange partners and fed more than 40 creative combinations through Automatic Creative Optimization. That testing discipline helped us find the mix that actually converted, cutting CPA by 34% while lifting installs and purchase rates.

**Proposed labels**
- Industry: retail, e-commerce
- Challenge: creative performance, user acquisition
- Channel: tiktok
- Region: americas
- Outcome: cost efficiency, creative effectiveness, test & learn

**Self-audit:** Removed influencers and paid social. The copy explicitly proves TikTok, but not enough to confidently classify influencer as a channel or paid social as a separate useful filter above TikTok itself.
