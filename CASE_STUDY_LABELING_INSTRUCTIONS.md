# Case Study Labeling Instructions

Purpose: assign filters with high precision so the app only surfaces genuinely relevant case studies.

Principles:
1. Evidence only. Use only what is explicitly supported by the case study copy (title, result, summary, story). Do not infer from adjacent wording, common agency practice, or client category assumptions.
2. Precision over recall. If a label is debatable, leave it off.
3. Multiple labels are allowed when the copy clearly supports them.
4. Industry should reflect the client's business category, not every adjacent descriptor.
5. Challenge should reflect the core marketing/business problem being solved, not every secondary effect.
6. Channel should include only channels clearly central to the execution. If the copy does not explicitly mention a channel, do not include it.
7. Region should reflect the markets explicitly named or clearly implied by the market field.
8. Outcome should capture what the case proves or delivers, based on the result and story.
9. Avoid duplicate concepts dressed in different names.
10. When uncertain, note the uncertainty and choose the narrower interpretation.

Taxonomy guidance:
- Industry: keep to 1-3 labels max in most cases.
- Challenge: keep to 1-3 labels max in most cases.
- Channel: keep all clearly relevant channels, but no implied ones.
- Outcome: keep to 1-4 labels max in most cases.

Audit requirement:
- After labeling each case study, perform a self-audit:
  - Is every label directly supported by the copy?
  - Did I add anything because it felt plausible rather than explicit?
  - Would this label help or hurt precision in a filter-based UI?
